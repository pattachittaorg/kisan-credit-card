Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme – Complete Guide for Farmers

Benefits, Eligibility, Interest Rates & Application Process 2025

The Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme is one of the most important financial initiatives launched by the Government of India to ensure easy and affordable credit access for farmers. Through this scheme, farmers can meet their agricultural expenses such as seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, irrigation, and allied activities without falling into the trap of high-interest informal loans.

Over the years, the KCC scheme has evolved into a comprehensive financial support system that not only provides short-term crop loans but also supports dairy farmers, fishermen, and animal husbandry professionals. This article explains everything you need to know about the kisan credit card scheme in a clear and farmer-friendly manner.

What Is Kisan Credit Card Scheme?

The Kisan Credit Card scheme was introduced in 1998 by the Government of India in collaboration with NABARD and public sector banks. Its primary objective is to provide farmers with timely access to credit at low interest rates, enabling them to manage agricultural operations smoothly throughout the cropping cycle.

Under this scheme, eligible farmers receive a credit card linked to their bank account. They can withdraw money as needed, repay after harvest, and reuse the credit limit without applying for a fresh loan each time.

Objectives of Kisan Credit Card

  • Provide hassle-free and timely credit to farmers
  • Reduce dependency on moneylenders
  • Support crop production and allied activities
  • Ensure financial inclusion of small and marginal farmers
  • Promote sustainable agricultural practices

Benefits of Kisan Credit Card Scheme

The KCC scheme offers several benefits that make it extremely popular among farmers across India.

  • Low interest rates with government subsidy
  • Flexible repayment options aligned with crop cycles
  • No collateral required for loans up to a specified limit
  • Coverage under crop insurance schemes
  • ATM-enabled card for easy withdrawals
Did you know? Farmers using KCC effectively can also improve soil productivity by investing in inputs recommended under the soil health card scheme.

Eligibility Criteria for Kisan Credit Card

The eligibility criteria for the KCC scheme are simple and inclusive, ensuring maximum coverage.

  • Individual farmers or joint borrowers
  • Small and marginal farmers
  • Tenant farmers and sharecroppers
  • Self Help Groups (SHGs) and Joint Liability Groups (JLGs)
  • Dairy, fisheries, and animal husbandry farmers

Documents Required for KCC Application

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Identity proof
  • Address proof
  • Land ownership or tenancy documents
  • Recent passport-size photographs

Interest Rate and Subsidy Details

One of the biggest advantages of the Kisan Credit Card scheme is its subsidized interest rate. Farmers usually receive crop loans at an interest rate of around 7%, which can be further reduced to as low as 4% with timely repayment.

This interest subvention makes KCC one of the most affordable credit options available for farmers in India.

How to Apply for Kisan Credit Card

Farmers can apply for a Kisan Credit Card both online and offline.

Offline Method

Visit the nearest bank branch, fill out the KCC application form, attach required documents, and submit it for verification.

Online Method

Many banks now offer online KCC applications through their official websites, making the process faster and more transparent.

Common Reasons for KCC Application Rejection

  • Incomplete documentation
  • Incorrect land records
  • Poor credit history
  • Mismatch in Aadhaar details

Conclusion

The Kisan Credit Card scheme plays a vital role in strengthening India’s agricultural economy by empowering farmers with affordable and timely credit. When used alongside government initiatives like crop insurance and soil testing programs, KCC helps farmers increase productivity and income sustainably.

Farmers are encouraged to apply for KCC, keep their records updated, and use the credit responsibly to achieve long-term financial stability and agricultural growth.
